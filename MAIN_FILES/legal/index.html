About Us

Aimed at promoting effective public participation through fighting idleness and disrespect together with communities.
The programme seeks to close the gap that exist between governement and communities.

Such is achived through:

  • Facilitating civic and public education to communities;
  • Rallying communities behind the implementation of strategic development plans such as; National Development Plan, PGDPs, IDPs and Community based plans;
  • Promoting effective public participation and
  • Enhancing sustainable community development through community-driven solution projects and programmes.

    • Moreover; the programme develops capacity for communities to understand “Who & How” their government works (systems and processes)

    Structure

    This is who we are, who we are going to be and what we want to do.

    Mission

    To establish Virtual States and enrol/harness the untapped skills and developmental potential of the unemployed members of the communities and; To minimize prevalence of idleness and disrespect in the community.

    Vision

    Patriotic communities working actively together with government on both the development and governance of our country.

    Objectives

    The strategic Objective is to empower the unemployed members of communities to play an active and volunteering role in addressing community problems:- By involving them in providing/facilitating public participation/education programmes on the functioning of government processes.

    V-Parliament

    The V-Parliament members are subjected to a series of public participation training programmes offered by the KwaZulu-Natal Legislature’s Public Participation and Petitions unit working closely with the Community. It refers to a forum where community members can engage in discussions, debates, and decision-making processes related to local governance and community issues. Similar to a traditional parliamentary setting, participants may have the opportunity to voice their opinions, propose ideas, and vote on matters that affect their community. The Virtual Parliament aims to emulate the functions of a real parliamentary body but in a setting that allows broader participation and accessibility.

    V-Cabinet

    Through its Rules, the V-Parliament nominates a Virtual Cabinet responsible for developing projects aimed at implementing V-Parliament resolutions. Virtual Cabinet is a advisory group comprised of community members, government officials, and stakeholders. The Virtual Cabinet serve as a platform for collaborative decision-making, policy development, and implementation oversight. Members of the Virtual Cabinet discuss priorities, review proposals, and provide input on matters such as local development plans, resource allocation, and community initiatives. This virtual structure aims to facilitate communication and cooperation between government and communities, ensuring that the voices and needs of citizens are considered in decision-making processes.

    Community Solution Projects

    CSPs serve as tools for community development and engagement, combating idleness and fostering productivity among members of the Virtual State. They encompass a wide range of activities, including physical projects like environmental clean-up efforts, as well as motivational and conceptual initiatives such as education workshops, research presentations, and substance abuse interventions. CSPs leverage the resources and skills of unemployed individuals within the community, aiming to address urgent development needs while also potentially leading to the creation of economic opportunities for participants.
